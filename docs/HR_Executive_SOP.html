Part A · Understand the Platform

A1. What LadderStep is

LadderStep Human Consulting is an end-to-end recruitment + HR outreach platform. It brings four kinds of users onto one system and moves a candidate from "sourced" all the way to "hired & paid for."

YOU

HR Executive

Source candidates, run cold outreach, manage leads, approve interviews & offers, raise invoices.

CLIENT

Hiring Company

Post jobs, browse talent, unlock resumes, request interviews & offers, pay fees.

TALENT

Candidate

Build a profile, apply to jobs, attend interviews, accept offers.

OVERSIGHT

Admin

Approve companies, assign companies to executives, set fee rates, oversee everything.

LIMITED

Trainer

Manages the training course catalogue only.

A2. The five portals & how they connect

Every portal talks to one shared server + database. Your role decides which portal you land in.

Admins can visit the HR Portal too. They see an "Admin View" banner with a "← Admin Dashboard" link to get back.

A3. How LadderStep earns money (know this early)

Candidates never pay. Companies pay in two ways — and your approvals unlock stream #2.
Your two approvals (interview & offer) are what secure the placement fee. That is why the system will not let a company schedule an interview or send an offer without you. Guard these gates.

B1. The login decision — one picture

One page, three doors. Nobody chooses their portal — the role behind the account decides.

B2. HR Executive & Admin — Microsoft 365

  1. Login page → Sign in with Microsoft.
  2. Use the Microsoft 365 account whose email matches the account an admin created for you.
  3. You land on the HR Dashboard (/hr); admins land on /admin.
🔒 No self-registration. A Microsoft sign-in with an unknown email is rejected — this stops strangers from creating privileged accounts. "Account not found" means an admin must add you first.

Provisioning a new executive (Admin does this)

Admin Portal → HR Staff Management → Add Staff → name + the exact email they will sign in to Microsoft with → the system creates a bare account and emails them → they click "Sign in with Microsoft" using that same email. Done.

B3. Candidate — Google (instant)

Login/Register → Sign in with Google → Google supplies verified name + email → first-timers are created and activated instantly → straight to /candidate. No approval needed.

Sourced candidates rarely log in. When you bulk-upload résumés (Part D), the system creates their account automatically, but they usually never sign in. That's expected — you act on their behalf.

B4. Company — Google + admin approval

🔒 A company cannot use the portal until an admin approves it. At approval the admin also sets whether they are Platinum (a contracted placement-fee %) — this drives all later fee maths (Part D6).

B5. Trainer — email + password

Login page → "Trainer? Sign in here" → email + password (set by the admin) → lands on Training Manager (/admin/training). This is the only role with a password.

Part C · The Complete Application Flow

This is the entire journey, from a cold prospect to a paid placement. Colour tells you who acts at each stage. Your two mandatory gates are stages 7 and 8.

1

Cold Outreach HR Executive

You email / WhatsApp / call prospective client companies from the Outreach module.

System: replies captured automatically → can become leads
2

Lead HR Executive

A reply becomes a Lead in your pipeline. You nurture it: new → contacted → interested → proposal → converted.

3

Company onboarded Company Admin

The lead signs up (Google). Admin approves them and sets their package / Platinum rate.

Admin also assigns the company to an executive (usually you)
4

Job posted Company

The company posts one or more job descriptions (JDs).

5

Sourcing HR Executive

You bulk-upload résumés against the JD or from the free pool. Each candidate gets a parsed profile and a fit score.

System: parses name, experience, education, skills; computes fit score
6

Discovery & interest Company

The company browses the Talent Pool. They either Unlock a candidate (Single/4-Pack) or Express Interest (→ lands in your Company Interests tab).

7

Interview — GATE 1 You approve

Company requests an interview → you approve → the slot is created. For sourced candidates you also confirm on their behalf.

🔒 Company cannot create slots directly — approval is mandatory
8

Offer — GATE 2 You approve

Company requests offer-letter release (with annual CTC) → you approve → offer letter is unlocked and the placement-fee invoice is raised (unless prepaid).

9

Hire Candidate

The candidate accepts. Status becomes hired → they are locked off the market everywhere.

10

Payment Company

The company pays the placement fee (Cashfree, full or partial). You track it on Offer Requests / Invoices.

D2. Cold Outreach (Outreach module)

  • Contact Lists: upload Excel/CSV. Rows with no email and no phone are skipped; duplicates removed.
  • Email Campaigns: personalise with merge tags — {{first_name}}, {{company_name}}, {{designation}}, {{city}}, {{executive_name}}. Sends 20 at a time; pausable.
  • WhatsApp: use Meta-approved templates, then a WhatsApp campaign.
  • Replies: reply inline, Convert to Lead, or Ignore. Cold Calls and Analytics round out the module.

D3. Lead pipeline — the stages

A lead can drop to "lost" from any stage. "Converted" is your sales win; the admin then onboards the company.

D4. Resume Sourcing — four tabs

1 · Upload Résumés

Two modes: 🎯 Target a Job (adds applicants + fit scores) or 🗂️ Free Pool (no JD). Up to 20 files, PDF/DOC/DOCX, 5 MB each.

2 · Free Pool

Every available candidate, independent of any job. Search by skill/experience. Click a name → full profile. Hired candidates excluded.

3 · Assign from Pool

Pick a JD → assign pool candidates to it. Assigned ones show a fit-score badge + matched/missing skills.

4 · Company Interests

Company "Express Interest" requests land here. Review → assign to their JD → Assign & Notify. Blocked if candidate is hired.

What happens automatically to each uploaded résumé

📄 Name résumés for accurate parsing. Use Serial - Name - Designation - X Yrs Y Month.pdf, e.g. 93_-_Seshu_Adusumilli_-_Manager_HR_-_17_Yrs_0_Month.pdf. The system reads the candidate's name & total experience straight from the filename, which is far more reliable than scraping the document body.
What the company sees vs you: you always see full, unmasked details. The company sees masked data (initials, no contact) until they unlock the candidate (Part D7).

D5. Interview approval — GATE 1 (swimlane)

🔒 Companies cannot create interview slots. The only way a slot exists is your approval. You see a request if the company is assigned to you OR you sourced that candidate.
Confirm on behalf: sourced candidates never log in, so in Scheduled Interviews you confirm their slot for them. The view flags "never logged in" + "executive-sourced" and shows real contact details so you can reach them.

D6. Offer & placement fee — GATE 2

How the placement fee is calculated (at submit time)

PLATINUM

Contracted %

fee = annual CTC × (percent ÷ 100)

e.g. 8.33 % ≈ one month's salary

DEFAULT

No contracted rate

fee = (annual CTC ÷ 12) × multiplier

multiplier is in months (default = 1)

PREPAID

Single / 4-Pack unlock

Fee waived. No invoice, no fee messaging to the company.

Everything downstream reads the ANNUAL CTC. The company input, your Offer Requests screen, and admin screens all say "Annual CTC." The company then pays via Cashfree (full or partial) and you track it on Offer Requests / Invoices.

D7. What the company experiences — resume-unlock packages

Before a company can browse the Talent Pool or see match %, they must pick a package. Know these three tiers so you can advise clients:

₹999

Single

1 unlock credit. Reveals the real résumé + contact. No placement fee later (prepaid).

₹3,999

4-Pack

4 credits, no expiry. Same reveal as Single. No placement fee later (prepaid).

CONTRACTED %

Platinum

Free, unlimited unlocks — but résumé stays masked; reach the candidate through you; placement fee applies at hire.

D8. The "off the market" locks

These are automatic. If you can't act on a candidate, check whether one of these locks applies — you'll see "Hired — off market" / "Hired elsewhere — unavailable" badges.

E2. Do's & Don'ts

✅ Do

Name sourced résumés Serial - Name - Designation - X Yrs Y Month.

Approve interview & offer requests promptly.

Use "Confirm on behalf" for sourced candidates.

Convert genuine outreach replies into leads.

Open "View Profile" before assigning to a JD.

❌ Don't

Promise a company they can self-schedule interviews or offers — the system blocks it.

Re-source a candidate who is hired or has a pending offer.

Share contact details of a candidate the company hasn't unlocked.

Quote placement fees from memory — the app computes them from annual CTC.
